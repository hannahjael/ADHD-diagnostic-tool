About ADHD

ADHD ‚Äì also referred to as attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder ‚Äì constitutes a diverse group of conditions related to development of the brain.

  • About 1 in 100 children has ADHD.
  • Characteristics may be detected in early childhood, but ADHD is often not diagnosed until much later.
  • The abilities and needs of people with ADHD vary and can evolve over time.
  • While some people with ADHD can live independently, others have severe disabilities and require life-long care and support.

Evidence-based psychosocial interventions can improve communication and social skills, with a positive impact on the well-being and quality of life of both people with ADHD and their caregivers.

Care for people with ADHD needs to be accompanied by actions at community and societal levels for greater accessibility, inclusivity and support.
